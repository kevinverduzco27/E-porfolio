About Me

My background, my motivation, and the path that led me to engineering.

My story begins in first grade, when I was taken out of recess and placed into a classroom that highlighted the difference between myself and my peers. Learning English was difficult, but through that struggle I developed resilience. Over time, I came to appreciate the unique perspective it gave me. Seeing the world through another language taught me adaptability, patience, and persistence.

Education, however, was not always easily accessible for my family. I often had to follow whatever path was available rather than the one I hoped for. Watching other students walk through opportunities I could only dream of was discouraging, but it also shaped how I approached challenges. I learned to recognize opportunity when it appeared and to commit fully when it did.

When I first attended university, I realized I had taken a step that was too large, too quickly. I felt unprepared and behind. Instead of giving up, I stepped back, entered the workforce, and saved every dollar I could to return to school. That decision taught me discipline and reinforced the value of patience and preparation. When I returned, I did so with confidence and purpose.

I am now proud to be graduating with honors in a field I am passionate about: engineering. I am preparing to transfer to a university to continue my education in Computer Engineering, where I can deepen my understanding of systems that merge hardware and software.

Passion for Engineering & Robotics

My interest in engineering, particularly robotics, comes from the ability to turn ideas into real, functional systems. I enjoy working on projects where software and hardware interact—whether that means designing digital logic, writing low-level programs, or building tools that solve tangible problems.

Advancing into higher education excites me because it means my projects can move beyond conceptual exercises and become more complex, complete, and impactful. Robotics represents this perfectly: it challenges me to think critically across disciplines while rewarding careful design and iteration.

Family & Motivation

I am the oldest of three brothers in an immigrant family. Growing up, I became aware early on that access to resources and opportunities is not evenly distributed. This awareness motivates much of what I do today.

My goal is to ensure that my brothers—and others like them—are not limited by a lack of options, but empowered by the choices they are able to make. Through education, engineering, and mentorship, I hope not only to walk through doors that once felt closed, but to help build new ones for those who come after me.
